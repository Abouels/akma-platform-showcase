# Version History

This document summarises key milestones in the evolution of the AKMA platform. The version numbers are approximate and reflect design and prototyping phases rather than formal software releases.

## v0.1 – Concept & requirements (Jan‑Feb 2024)

* Translated the real business needs of a construction and finishing company into high‑level requirements.
* Defined the core modules: projects, BoQs, procurement, warehousing, finance, reporting and user management.
* Outlined the main workflows (purchase cycle, BoQ lifecycle, stock management, financial entries) and created initial wireframes and flowcharts.

## v0.2 – AI‑prototyped modules (Mar 2024)

* Used prompt‑driven AI tools to generate working prototypes of the main screens and data structures.
* Built base functionality for projects, BoQs, purchase requests, purchase orders and goods receipts.
* Started internal testing with dummy data and iterated quickly based on feedback.

## v0.3 – Multi‑language & dashboards (Apr 2024)

* Added bilingual support (Arabic/English) across all screens and print templates.
* Implemented project dashboards showing key metrics, budget utilisation and procurement status.
* Introduced basic role‑based permissions (administrator, project manager, procurement officer, warehouse manager, finance, viewer).

## v1.0 – Minimum Viable Product (May 2024)

* Completed core workflow integration: purchase requests convert to orders; goods receipts update stock; invoices feed into finance module.
* Added BoQ revision management and linking to procurement and stock usage.
* Developed standard reports and printouts (POs, receipts, BoQs, invoices).

## v1.1 – Enhanced roles & reporting (Jun‑Jul 2024)

* Refined the permissions matrix and added granular user roles.
* Improved reporting filters and export to Excel/PDF. Added income/expense and cash flow reports.
* Added re‑order suggestions based on minimum stock levels.

## v1.2 – Operational rollout & feedback (Aug 2024 onwards)

* Deployed the system in a pilot environment with real company data (projects, vendors, warehouses and financial records).
* Collected feedback from users (project managers, procurement, warehouse and finance teams) and iterated on usability, performance and print layouts.
* Planned future enhancements such as mobile access, analytics and integration with accounting software.

These milestones illustrate how the platform matured from an idea to a functional system through iterative prototyping, AI‑assisted development and close collaboration with stakeholders.
